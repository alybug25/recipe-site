Pancakes

A classic breakfast option. You can eat them with maple syrup and bacon, a chocolate spread, peanut butter, fresh fruit - the sky's the limit! Avoid over-mixing the batter and leave it to rest for a few minutes after mixing to help it become thicker. This way, it won't spread out too much and hold its shape better.

Prep - 15 mins Cook - 30 mins Serving - serves 4
*please note: these are just rough estimates and will vary. happy cooking!

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 130ml milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter (and a little extra for greasing the pan)

Equipment

  • measuring cups and jug
  • bowl
  • whisk
  • frying pan
  • ladle and spatula

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix all the dry ingredients together.
  2. Add the egg and melted butter and mix until mostly incorporated.
  3. In approximate thirds, stir in the milk until the pancake batter is homogenous.
  4. Heat the frying pan over the stove at medium-high heat.
  5. Grease the pan by swirling some butter around so it melts and covers the pan's surface.
  6. Using a ladle (or measuring cup, whatever you have on hand) to scoop some batter into the pan.
  7. When bubbles form on the top of the pancake and the edges are defined (about 2-3 minutes), flip it.
  8. Cook the other side for another 2-3 mins, then serve and enjoy!
